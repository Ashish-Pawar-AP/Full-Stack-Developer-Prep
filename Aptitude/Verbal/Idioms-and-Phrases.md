# 📘 Aptitude Verbal – Idioms & Phrases  
(Level-wise: Easy, Medium, Hard, Very Hard)  
Includes meanings + short examples (important for exams & placements)

---

# 🟢 EASY LEVEL (1–10)

---

## 1️⃣ A piece of cake  
**Meaning:** Very easy  
**Example:** The test was a piece of cake.

---

## 2️⃣ Break the ice  
**Meaning:** Start a conversation  
**Example:** He told a joke to break the ice.

---

## 3️⃣ Once in a blue moon  
**Meaning:** Very rarely  
**Example:** I eat junk food once in a blue moon.

---

## 4️⃣ Hit the nail on the head  
**Meaning:** Say exactly the right thing  
**Example:** She hit the nail on the head.

---

## 5️⃣ Under the weather  
**Meaning:** Feeling sick  
**Example:** I am feeling under the weather today.

---

## 6️⃣ A blessing in disguise  
**Meaning:** Something good that seemed bad  
**Example:** Losing that job was a blessing in disguise.

---

## 7️⃣ In hot water  
**Meaning:** In trouble  
**Example:** He is in hot water for being late.

---

## 8️⃣ Spill the beans  
**Meaning:** Reveal a secret  
**Example:** She spilled the beans about the surprise.

---

## 9️⃣ The ball is in your court  
**Meaning:** It’s your turn to act  
**Example:** I have done my part; now the ball is in your court.

---

## 🔟 Kill two birds with one stone  
**Meaning:** Solve two problems at once  
**Example:** I killed two birds with one stone by studying while traveling.
